Paneer Butter Massala

Source: Indian Healthy Recipes

 

1 tbsp oil

3/4 inch ginger

3 medium garlic cloves

2 green cardamons (or ground cardamon)

3 medium onions

1 can diced tomatoes

3/4 tbsp sugar

3/4 tbsp salt

12 to 14 cashew nuts (or 12 almonds)

1 tsp red chili powder (or paprika, for color)

1 tsp Garam Masala

1 tsp ground coriander

1 cup water

 

1 small bay leaf

1/2 inch cinammon piece

2 green cardamon

 

25g butter

1/2 cup water

250g paneer, cubed

1 tsp kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)

3 tbsp cream (soy is fine)

2 tbsp cilantro leaves (for garnish)

 

Start cooking the rice (if whole). If using store-bought paneer, soak it in hot water for 20 minutes.

 

1. Heat oil, add ginger, garlic cloves, cardamon, onions. Stir fry until transparent to light pink.

2. Add tomatoes and salt. Simmer for 3 minutes. Cover and cook until the onions and tomatoes turn soft and completely mushy.

3. Add everything up to coriander. Stir fry until the mixture turns aromatic.

4. Transfer to blender, pour water, blend to a really silky smooth puree.

 

1. Heat butter in the same pan. When melted, add any and all of the 3 spices you have.

2. Add the puree, taste test (ex: more salt, or more paprika for color).

3. Pour more water, still well, bring to a roiling boil. Reduce to medium and cook, covered, until the gravy turns thicker. Keep stirring every now and then.

3. Taste test, add Garam Masala if needed.

4. Lower the flame completely, add cubed paneer. Take karusi methi between your palms and crush it. Add it.

5. Mix and cook covered on low heat for 2 minutes. Turn off and pour cream.

5. Garnish with coriander leaves (if any). Serve with butter naan or Basmati rice.

Bean Bolognese

Source : Eating Well

Serves 4

 

 

1 14-ounce can salad beans or other beans (see Tip), rinsed, divided

2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil

1 small onion, chopped

1/2 cup chopped carrot

1/4 cup chopped celery

1/2 teaspoon salt

4 cloves garlic, chopped

1 bay leaf

1/2 cup white wine

1 14-ounce can diced tomatoes

1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley, divided

8 ounces whole-wheat fettuccine

1/2 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese

 

1. When adding wine in the following section, boil water and drop pasta about 9 minutes until just tender. Drain.

2. Heat oil in a medium saucepan over medium heat. Add onion, carrot, celery and salt; cover and cook, stirring occasionally, until softened, about 10 minutes. Add garlic and bay leaf; cook, stirring, until fragrant, about 15 seconds. Add wine; increase heat to high and boil until most of the liquid evaporates, 3 to 4 minutes. Mash 1/2 cup beans in a small bowl with a fork, and add with tomatoes (with juices) and some parsley. Bring to a lively simmer and cook, stirring occasionally, until thickened, about 6 minutes. Add the remaining whole beans; cook, stirring occasionally, until heated through, 1 to 2 minutes more. Remove the bay leaf from the sauce.

Divide the pasta among 4 bowls. Top with the sauce, sprinkle with Parmesan and the remaining parsley.

1 onion, slices

2 garlic cloves, chopped

1 ½ tsp dried oregano leaves

1/8 tsp ground cinnamon

15oz can diced tomatoes

1 tbsp capers

1 tsp honey

1 large cauliflower head, broken into flowerets

½ lemon, juice

2 to 4 oz feta cheese, crumbled

Parsley, chopped

 

Preheat the broiler. Lightly oil a 2-quart gratin or casserole dish.

Source: Eating Well

Serves 4

 

2 ounces pancetta, chopped

3 cloves garlic, minced

2 large tomatoes, chopped

1/2 teaspoon sugar

1/4 teaspoon crushed red pepper

2 teaspoons red-wine vinegar

1/4 teaspoon salt

1 pound gnocchi (see Shopping Tip)

4 ounces watercress, tough stems removed, coarsely chopped (6 cups packed)

1/3 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese

 

1. Put a large pan of water on to boil.

2. Cook pancetta in a large nonstick skillet over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until it begins to brown, 4 to 5 minutes. Add garlic and cook, stirring, for 30 seconds. Chocolate crinkles

Serves: 36

 

4 tablespoons (56 grams) unsalted butter

8 ounces (225 grams) semisweet or bittersweet chocolate, coarsely chopped

1/2 cup (100 grams) granulated white sugar

2 large eggs

2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract

1 1/2 cups (210 grams) all-purpose flour

1/4 teaspoon salt

1/2 teaspoon baking powder

 

Topping:

1 cup (110 grams) confectioners (powdered or icing) sugar, sifted

 

In a stainless steel bowl, over a saucepan of simmering water, melt the chocolate and butter. Remove from heat and set aside.

 

In the bowl of your electric mixer (or with a hand mixer), beat the eggs and sugar until thick, pale, and fluffy. (When you slowly raise the beaters the batter will fall back into the bowl in slow ribbons.) At this point beat in the vanilla extract and then stir in the melted chocolate mixture.

 

In a separate bowl whisk together the flour, salt, and baking powder. Add dry ingredients to the chocolate mixture, stirring just until incorporated. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate until firm enough to shape into balls, at least 1 hour (preferably several hours or even overnight).

 

Preheat oven to 325 degrees F (170 degrees C) and place rack in center of oven. Line two baking sheets with parchment paper, and set aside.

 

Place the confectioner’s sugar in a shallow bowl. With lightly greased hands, roll a small amount of chilled dough to form a 1 inch (2.54 cm) diameter ball. Place the ball of dough into the confectioner’s sugar and roll the ball in the sugar until it is completely coated and no chocolate shows through. Gently lift the sugar-covered ball, tapping off excess sugar, and place on prepared baking sheet. Continue forming cookies, spacing about 2 inches (5 cm) apart on baking sheets. (If you find the dough getting too soft for rolling into balls, return to the refrigerator and let chill until firm.)

 

Bake cookies for 10 to 13 minutes or just until the edges are slightly firm but the centers are still soft. (For moist chewy cookies do not over bake. Over baking these cookies will cause them to be dry.) Remove from oven and place on a wire rack to cool.

 

These cookies are best eaten the day they are baked.

Lattice Linzer Torte

Source: Family Fun magazine

Dough

¾ cup blanched almonds

1¾ cups all purpose flour

½ + 1/3 cup confectioner’s sugar

¼ tsp cinnamon

1/8 tsp ground cloves

1/8 tsp salt

9 tbsp unsalted butter, cold, cut into ½ inch chunks

1 large egg, cold

½ tsp vanilla extract

¼ tsp almond extract

Finely grind the almonds. Mix all dry ingredients at low speed until mixture looks pebbly. Add egg and extracts and mix until dough starts to form.

On lightly floured surface, gently knead dough until it comes together (do not overknead). Divide in two pieces, one twice as large as the other, and shape into disks. Wrap each in plastic and refrigerate for several hours or overnight.

 

Filling

1 cup raspberry preserves (variation: or other)

1 large egg, cold

1 pinch of salt

¼ cup sliced almonds

Heat over to 375. Lightly butter a 9-inch tart pan with removable bottom.

On lightly floured surface, roll the larger disk of dough into a 12-inch circle, about ¼ inch thick. Fit the dough into the tart pan, pressing it into the corners and sides. If the dough becomes too soft for handling, freeze it for a few minutes.

Spread the raspberry preserves evenly over the bottom of the tart shell.

Roll the remaining disk into a 10-inch circle, about ¼ inch thick. Use knife to cut the dought into ten strips, about ¾ inch wide, and carefully lay five strips evenly spaced. Press the ends of each strip into the edges of the crust. Lay the five remaining strips perpendicularly. Chill for at least 15 minutes before baking.

Whisk the egg with a pinch of salt. Brush the egg over the lattic and crust, then sprinkle with almonds. Bake for 30 – 40 minutes or until the filling bubbles and the pastry turns golden brown.

Cool completely on a rack before unmolding and serving. Store, covered, in the refrigerator for up to a week or freezer up to a month.
